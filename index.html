About Me

I am a Computer Science and Engineering Ph.D. student at The Pennsylvania State University. I graduated with a B.S. in High Honors from Department of Computer Engineering, Bilkent University, Ankara, Turkey. My research focuses on improving deep learning inference for autonomous driving, emphasizing speed, energy efficiency, and scheduling optimizations. Previously, I worked on portrait drawing using Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). Outside of research, I enjoy exploring advancements in immersive technologies and optimization techniques.

Projects

Current Research

Enhancing the perception pipeline for autonomous driving, optimizing real-time performance to meet strict deadlines in both 2D and 3D detection tasks. My work includes exploring sensor fusion and efficient scheduling strategies for multiple deep learning models in real-time systems. I am using the Autoware simulator together with AWSIM to explore ideas on pipeline optimization and inference scheduling.

AR/VR Pipeline Exploration

Improved performance features for immersive experiences. This work involved optimizing rendering and processing pipelines, specifically focusing on the effect of camera orientation on Asynchronous Timewarp (ATW) using the ILLIXR testbed to reduce latency and improve visual consistency.

Portrait Drawing with GANs

Developed unsupervised learning techniques to train a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) to draw portraits. The model was trained on various datasets to understand artistic features and create lifelike representations.
